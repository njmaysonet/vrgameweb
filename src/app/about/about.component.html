About the Project

The UCF Cultural VR Project's goal is to design a virtual reality video game that teaches players about different cultures and also asks them to go through possible real life scenarios concerning acceptable behaviors and norms present in that culture.

As such we have decided to create a web platform from which users can find out more information about the game and look up their user profiles to look over their progress. This is supported by a database that collects information about how users acted in their play sessions to allow study of how users made their choices and what they learned.

We utilize the Unity©, HTC Vive© and SteamVR© technologies for game development as well as NodeJS, Express, Angular, and MySQL to run the website front-end and back-end.

Our Sponsor

Dr. Emily Johnson UCF Games Research Lab

Personal Statement

Dr. Emily K. Johnson, a postdoctoral research associate coordinating the Games Research Lab, is a recent graduate of the Texts & Technology Ph.D. program at UCF. Her dissertation, “Making Waves, Mixing Colors, and Using Mirrors: The Self-regulated Learning Support Features and Procedural Rhetoric of Three Whole-Body Educational Games” incorporates research from several fields including game studies, literacy studies, education, embodied learning, and psychology to examine the ways that these types of games teach science content, scaffold self-regulated learning skills, and reinforce specific playing techniques and cultural and pedagogical values. Emily is a former middle school teacher who is passionate about research, technology, learning, and fun. Her research interests include: Educational Technology, Simulations and Learning, Self-Regulated Learning, Gameful Learning, Learning and Motivation, In Situ/Stealth Assessments, Self-Efficacy and Learning, and Oral History.
